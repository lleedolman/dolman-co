Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  ABN 46 775 837 936

Dolman & Co. (ABN 46 775 837 936) is committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose your personal information.

1. Who We Are

Dolman & Co. is a registered trade mark attorney practice operating in Australia. References to "we", "us", or "our" in this policy refer to Dolman & Co. (ABN 46 775 837 936). Our principal contact for privacy matters is trademarks@dolman.com.au.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Your name, email address, and phone number when you contact us or book a consultation
  • Business details including ABN, company name, and trading name
  • Information about your trade marks, brand, or intellectual property matters you share with us
  • Payment information processed through secure third-party payment providers
  • Website usage data collected through cookies and analytics tools

We only collect information that is reasonably necessary for the provision of our services or for responding to your enquiry.

3. How We Collect Your Information

We collect personal information directly from you when you:

  • Contact us via email, phone, or through our website
  • Book a consultation or engage our services
  • Provide documents or information relevant to your IP matter
  • Subscribe to any communications from us

We may also collect information from publicly available sources such as IP Australia's trade mark register, ASIC's company register, and court or tribunal records where relevant to your matter.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide trade mark attorney services and IP advice
  • Communicate with you about your matter
  • Fulfil our obligations to IP Australia and other regulatory bodies
  • Send you relevant updates about your trade mark registrations or renewals
  • Comply with our legal and professional obligations
  • Improve our services and website

We will not use your information for unrelated purposes without your consent.

5. Disclosure of Your Information

We may disclose your personal information to:

  • IP Australia for the purpose of filing and prosecuting trade mark applications
  • Third-party service providers who assist us in operating our practice (such as accounting software, file storage, and communication tools), who are bound by confidentiality obligations
  • Courts, tribunals, or regulatory bodies where required by law
  • Other professional advisers (such as solicitors) where you have instructed us to do so

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

6. International Disclosure

Where you instruct us to file trade marks internationally (for example through the Madrid Protocol), your information will be disclosed to the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) and relevant overseas trade mark offices. We take reasonable steps to ensure such disclosures comply with Australian privacy law.

7. Storage & Security

We store personal information securely using reputable cloud-based services with appropriate encryption and access controls. We retain client files for a minimum of seven years following the conclusion of a matter, in accordance with professional obligations. We take reasonable steps to protect your information from misuse, loss, unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure.

8. Cookies & Website Analytics

Our website may use cookies and analytics tools to understand how visitors use our site. This information is aggregated and does not identify you personally. You may disable cookies through your browser settings, though this may affect website functionality.

9. Access & Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request corrections if that information is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date. To make a request, contact us at trademarks@dolman.com.au. We will respond within 30 days. In some circumstances we may be required by law to withhold certain information.

10. Complaints

If you believe we have handled your personal information in a way that does not comply with the Australian Privacy Principles, please contact us in the first instance at trademarks@dolman.com.au. We will investigate and respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be available on our website at dolman.com.au. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
